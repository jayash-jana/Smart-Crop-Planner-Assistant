Protect Soil with Cover Crops

Cover crops (also called "green manure") are plants grown specifically to improve soil between main crops.

Benefits:

  • Prevent erosion
  • Suppress weeds
  • Add organic matter
  • Fix nitrogen (legumes)
  • Break up compacted soil

Best Cover Crops:

  • Winter rye (cold-hardy)
  • Crimson clover (nitrogen-fixing)
  • Buckwheat (quick summer cover)
  • Hairy vetch (excellent nitrogen)

Video: "Cover Crops for Soil Health" (7:20)

5

Practice No-Till Methods

Excessive tilling destroys soil structure, disrupts fungal networks, and speeds up organic matter decomposition.

üå± No-Till Approaches:

  • Use a shovel or rake to scratch in compost - no deep tillage needed
  • Leave plant roots in ground after harvesting
  • Use mulching mower that returns clippings to soil
  • Mulch fall leaves directly into lawn rather than bagging

üìä Results of No-Till:

Better soil structure, increased organic matter, improved water infiltration, enhanced microbial activity, less work for you!
